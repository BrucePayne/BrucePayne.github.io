Explaination of Football Predictions
 

Team
The team listed in the left most column is the higher ranked team (not necessarily the favorate).

Line
The national line(Line) that used here can be found at http://www.geocities.com/Colosseum/Arena/2460/odds.htm. and may change through the week (after I retrieve it). If a different line is desired simply compare the Wavg column with the different line to determine the predicted winner. A negative line indicates that Team is favored to beat Opponent by that many points. A poisitive line indicates that Opponent is favored to beat Team by that many points.

Opponent
The team listed in the third column is the opponent of the Team listed in the first column. This team is usually the underdog, but not always.

Actual
This column is only present on the results pages. It is the actual difference in the scores (Opponent's score - Team's score)..

Wavg
This column is my predicted spread - the sign convention is the same as Line. The weighted average(Wavg) is computed using 10 different prediction algorithms. The weights assigned to each algorithm are based on the performance history of each algorithm so far this season, with the most recent weeks being considered more that older weeks.

Star Picks
The algorithm to determine Star Picks varies from week to week, but generally it is based on the standard deviation of the different prediction algorithms, the difference between the weighted average(Wavg) and the national line(Line), and/or the absolute value of the national line. One, two, and three star picks are possible to this point, but I am considering expanding that to include four and maybe even five star picks. Star picks are indicated by *'s in front of the Pick.

Pick
The pick is the team I predict to win against the spread. If the Wavg is less than Line this will be Team, otherwise it will be Opponent. On results pages correct picks are bold.
